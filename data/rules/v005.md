# Prediction Rules v005

## Objective And Horizon
- Predict only the next trading session's close-to-close direction.
- `prob_up` is the probability that the stock closes higher next session.
- `prob_up > 0.50` is an up prediction; `prob_up < 0.50` is a down prediction.
- If duplicate same-ticker/session predictions disagree and the averaged directional gap is below `0.04`, force final `prob_up` to `0.50-0.51`.
- Do not use quarterly, annual, strategic, or long-dated narratives unless they plausibly change next-session positioning.
- Never recommend margin, leverage, borrowing, cash advances, or trades requiring debt. User-facing prediction text must carry `Not financial advice.`

## Performance-Grounded Priors (read first)
This review period scored 42.0% hits with a coin-flip Brier (0.2512). The system has NO general directional edge; profit came almost entirely from a few large-magnitude down winners. Therefore:
1. The default for any setup without a demonstrated edge (below) is `0.50`, not a marginal tilt.
2. There are exactly three demonstrated edges this period, and they define when to leave neutral:
   - **Fresh-negative-catalyst US single-name shorts** (dilution/offering, post-catalyst fade with confirming downside volume, SpaceX-IPO liquidity-drain space names). These were the reliable hits (PL, LUNR, RKLB, SERV, SMR, USAR, ASTS, SNDK, SITM, KLAC).
   - **Single-name exhaustion fades of extended US names on a NEUTRAL broad tape** (failed gap-ups with weak close), e.g. the 06-16 cohort AAOI/AOSL/AMBA/AMKR/AXTI.
   - **Up/neutral calls on Chinese/Asian names when the US semi tape is supportive** (these hit repeatedly: 002281, 300394, 300502, 600584, 6488, 688082).
3. Three demonstrated anti-edges (these are now hard gates, below): Asian/China down-calls from US-tape read-through; continuation down-calls after a broad washout; up-tilts on extended US semis merely because breadth blocked a short.

## Evidence Hierarchy
1. Rank evidence in this order:
   - Fresh company-specific earnings, guidance, IPO/secondary-offering liquidity drain, lock-up supply, offering/dilution, contract/order size, regulatory order/approval, financing, buyback/dividend, or confirmed financial impact.
   - Regular-session reaction to that catalyst.
   - Direct peer earnings/guidance read-through.
   - Broad market, sector ETF, and sector-leader regime.
   - Stock-specific price action and volume.
   - Macro/rates/futures/geopolitical backdrop.
   - Analyst notes, product launches, partnerships, listicles, institutional-positioning headlines, and theme articles.
2. Product launches, partnerships, MOUs, conferences, index chatter, analyst target hikes, "AI beneficiary" articles, and political-trading headlines are soft inputs unless they include disclosed near-term revenue, order size, margin, guidance, dilution, or regulatory financial impact.
3. Duplicate articles about one event count once.
4. A stale positive narrative plus a strong candle is not a fresh catalyst.

## Geography Rules (China / Hong Kong / Asia) — HARD GATES
These supersede every bearish gate below for the named geographies.
1. **Asian semi down-call ban from foreign-tape read-through.** For any A-share (`.SZ`/`.SS`), Hong Kong (`.HK`), Taiwan (`.TW`/`.TWO`), or Japan (`.T`) semiconductor / AI-infra / optical name, a hostile US or global peer tape is NOT a valid basis for a down call. Force `prob_up >= 0.50`. (06-17: down-calls justified by US peer median −4.9% hit ~2/13; 002281 +10.0%, 300308 +7.2%, 603986 +7.3%, 300502 +4.2%, 6488 +0.9%, 6669 +1.0%.)
2. **Chinese down-call ban (general).** For any A-share or HK-listed name with no fresh same-day negative hard catalyst, do NOT issue a down call — force `prob_up >= 0.50` regardless of extension, failed gap-up, parabolic, or exhaustion reasoning. Domestic-substitution flows make these decouple upward and squeeze shorts.
3. A Chinese/HK down call is permitted ONLY with a fresh, same-day, company-specific negative hard catalyst (confirmed dilution/offering, regulatory order, earnings miss that traded). Parabolic extension, fading volume, failed gap-ups, and stale narrative do not qualify.
4. Up/neutral calls on Asian names are allowed: when the US semi tape is supportive, a confirming-up Asian close justifies `0.50-0.52` (capped). When the live/overnight quote is already green for the next session, treat that as mechanical support for a green close-to-close.
5. Japanese parabolic semis (e.g. 4062.T, 4063.T) MAY fade on their own low-volume failed gap-ups when the US tape is neutral-to-down — but only to `0.49` (minimal tilt), never lower, because of squeeze history.

## Broad-Regime Gates — HARD GATES
1. **Washout-bounce gate (new).** If the most recent completed session was a broad risk-off / washout day — AI-infra/semi peer median `<= -3%`, OR broad indices and sector ETFs broadly down with elevated VIX — do NOT issue continuation down-calls on semi/AI/high-beta names for the next session unless the name has a fresh same-day negative hard catalyst. Force `prob_up >= 0.50`. The oversold bounce dominates. (06-17 → 06-18: ACMR +14.2%, AOSL +10.2%, ASX +8.3%, AMAT +7.3%, AMD +4.9%, AMKR +4.7%, AVGO +4.7%, ARM +5.1%, ASML +3.2% — near-total wipeout of fade-down calls.)
2. **Mean-reversion broad-up gate (retained).** When the sector ETF is up more than `2%` OR more than `65%` of the universe closed green, force `prob_up` to `0.50-0.51` for ANY down call lacking a fresh same-day negative hard catalyst.
3. **Distinguish exhaustion fades from washout continuation.** A failed-gap-up fade of one extended name is valid ONLY on a neutral broad tape (06-16 cohort hit). The same fade is invalid the session after a broad washout (06-18 cohort lost). When in doubt about which regime you are in, force `0.50`.

## Hard Catalyst Rules
1. A hard catalyst is active for three trading sessions (Day 0-1 primary, Day 2-3 secondary, after Day 3 stale).
2. For a fresh untraded positive hard catalyst, start at `prob_up = 0.54`.
3. Allow `0.55-0.60` only if catalyst is company-specific and measurable, regular/premarket price action confirms, and volume is at least `1.2x` average or sector breadth confirms.
4. If a positive catalyst has already traded and the stock closes below the open or in the bottom 40% of range, classify `post-catalyst fade`; cap `prob_up` at `0.50`.
5. If a stock gained more than `8%` on Day 0-1 after a positive catalyst with no new after-close update, cap continuation at `0.51`; prefer `0.47-0.50` only if the next session closes weak AND the broad tape is not a washout (per Broad-Regime Gate 1).
6. **Fresh negative catalysts with confirmed weak price action are the most reliable short signal and the strongest demonstrated edge.** Use `prob_up = 0.44-0.48` for (US/non-Chinese names only):
   - Secondary offerings, resale registrations, insider-selling offerings, or dilution.
   - Earnings beats that sell off on guidance/margin/AI-outlook/expectation disappointment.
   - Direct peer earnings/guidance that damages the thesis.
   - Regulatory/legal events with plausible near-term financial impact.
   - SpaceX-IPO liquidity-drain space names (ASTS/RKLB/LUNR/PL) on confirming downside price action.
7. If a fresh negative catalyst is already followed by an extended-down move, do not go below `0.44` unless volume is extreme and sector/macro is also hostile.
8. **Dilution decay.** Once a negative offering/dilution catalyst enters Day 2-3 and the stock is already `extended down`, floor `prob_up` at `0.48` and cap confidence at `0.50`; the easy move has traded and squeeze risk dominates (SMCI/ONDS/PL Day-2/3 shorts repeatedly failed).

## Soft Catalyst Rules
1. Soft-catalyst-only predictions must stay in `0.48-0.52`.
2. A soft positive headline plus top-quartile close may justify only `0.51-0.52`.
3. A soft positive headline plus extension up or low volume must not justify `prob_up > 0.51`.
4. Analyst target hikes, top-pick calls, product PR, conference participation, partnerships without financial scale, and listicles may move probability by at most 1 point.
5. Do not assign `0.53+` unless there is a fresh hard catalyst or strong sector breadth plus confirming price and volume.
6. If news is stale/thematic and the latest close is weak, prefer `0.47-0.49` — but only for US/non-Chinese names and only on a non-washout tape.

## Price Action
1. A top-quartile close is bullish only when close-to-close is positive and volume is at least neutral.
2. A low-volume top-quartile close after a selloff is only a stabilization signal; cap at `0.51`.
3. A bottom-quartile close is bearish only when close-to-close is negative or the stock failed a gap-up.
4. A failed gap-up: open above prior close, intraday high above open, close below open or in bottom half of range.
5. **Failed gap-up gate.** A failed gap-up with no fresh hard catalyst is a gate against an up call: use `0.47-0.50`, moving below `0.47` only when downside volume or hostile breadth confirms. This gate is OVERRIDDEN (forced back to `>=0.50`) by: the Chinese/Asian geography gates, the washout-bounce gate, and the broad-up mean-reversion gate. On US names on a neutral tape, the failed-gap fade is a valid edge.
6. A single strong close after stale news does not override extension, weak macro, or a recent post-catalyst fade.

## Extension And Mean Reversion
1. `extended up`: gain above `8%` over 3 sessions or above `15%` over 5 sessions.
2. `extended down`: loss below `-6%` over 3 sessions or below `-12%` over 5 sessions.
3. Extension alone is not a signal; extension plus weak price action plus a non-washout tape is.
4. Extended-up US name, no fresh hard catalyst:
   - Strong close and confirming volume: `0.50`.
   - Weak close or failed gap-up, neutral tape: `0.46-0.49`.
   - Weak close but day after a broad washout: force `0.50` (bounce gate).
5. Extended-down, no fresh negative hard catalyst:
   - Do not automatically predict rebound, but never short it either — `0.50-0.52`.
   - `0.51-0.52` only on a top-quartile close with at least neutral volume.
6. Asian/A-share/HK parabolic moves are governed entirely by the Geography Rules — do NOT fade them.

## Sector And Peer Breadth
1. Sector breadth can prevent an aggressive contrarian call but cannot by itself justify a bullish call above `0.52`.
2. Use sector breadth only when at least two are known: same-group median, sector ETF, direct leader, or direct peer read-through.
3. **Same-day breadth does not predict next-session direction for extended names.** When strong same-day breadth suppresses a short on an extended-up US name, set the call to exactly `0.50` (pure neutral) — do NOT tilt up. (06-15: breadth-floored "up 0.50" calls on LITE/MRVL/MU/AMD/LRCX/ONTO/SMTC/AAOI/SMCI all fell 4-10% next session.)
4. Strong-breadth short-suppression (`>= +3%` SOXX or two strongly-positive indicators, no fresh negative catalyst) forces `prob_up >= 0.50` and supersedes failed-gap/extension fade gates — but the resulting call is `0.50`, never higher, unless the name's own price AND volume also confirm up.
5. Disable any bullish sector-breadth override during hostile/washout regime sessions.
6. Direct peer read-through is actionable only with clear business linkage:
   - Memory/storage: MU, WDC, STX, SNDK.
   - AI servers: DELL, SMCI, HPE.
   - Semicap: AMAT, ASML, KLAC, LRCX, ONTO, CAMT.
   - Optics/connectivity: CRDO, COHR, LITE, AAOI, CIEN.
   - AI data-center power: VRT, ETN, GEV, VST, CEG.
7. A negative US peer read-through never justifies a down call on a Chinese/HK/Asian name (Geography Gate 1).
8. A single negative peer read-through cannot push `prob_up` below `0.49` for US names unless at least two breadth indicators are hostile.

## Macro And Regime
1. Macro alone should keep probabilities inside `0.47-0.53`.
2. Macro becomes actionable when at least two align: weak futures/broad tech selloff, hawkish rates, geopolitical risk-off, sector ETF/leader weakness, direct peer selloff.
3. In a hostile macro/sector regime, do not issue soft-catalyst up calls above `0.51`.
4. **A hostile/risk-off macro day is NOT a license to short the next session.** It triggers the washout-bounce gate, not continuation. Only a fresh same-day negative hard catalyst justifies a down call into the day after risk-off.
5. Exogenous macro/geopolitical one-off events (Iran peace, sanctions, oil shocks) provide at most ONE session of directional breadth support; from the second session on, force any call resting solely on the event to `0.50`.

## Volume
1. Elevated volume confirms the direction of a breakout, failed gap-up, breakdown, or post-catalyst reaction.
2. Low/uncertain volume caps `prob_up` between `0.49-0.51` unless a fresh hard catalyst exists.
3. Low-volume reversal-up after selloff is not enough for an up call above `0.51`.
4. Low-volume continuation after an extended rally is exhaustion risk — but a down call on this basis is valid ONLY for US names on a neutral tape (not Chinese, not post-washout).
5. Elevated downside volume after a failed gap-up supports `0.44-0.47` (US single-name fades).

## Probability Calibration
0. Use raw `prob_up` for all decisions and display; keep calibrated output disabled until separate up/down variants beat raw Brier on holdout across at least 3 scored sessions.
1. Most predictions should be `0.49-0.52`. Given the period's coin-flip Brier, when uncertain, choose `0.50`.
2. Use `0.51-0.52` for modest price-action or sector-only bullish edges.
3. Use `0.47-0.49` for US stale-narrative-plus-weak-price-action ONLY on a non-washout tape.
4. Use `0.44-0.47` only for the demonstrated edge: fresh negative hard catalyst / failed gap-up with confirming downside volume / liquidity-drain space name, on a US name, on a non-washout tape.
5. If fresh negative catalyst is `none` and preliminary `prob_up` is `0.46-0.49` only because of confirming down price action, hostile sector, or stale narrative, move final `prob_up` to `0.50-0.51` unless confirming downside volume AND two same-group breadth indicators are also down.
6. Direction-specific guardrail: for low-confidence near-neutral down calls with no hard catalyst, lift `prob_up` by 1-2 points; keep the down direction only when at least two of price action, volume, and sector breadth point down.
7. Use `0.55+` only for fresh measurable hard catalysts with confirmation and no extension/fade/washout warning.
8. Never use `0.55+` for stale AI, product, analyst, partnership, index, or theme headlines.
9. If evidence is mixed, stale, duplicated, low-volume, or conflicting, force `0.49-0.51`.
10. Direction-concentration gate: when more than `70%` of the session's universe points the same direction, force any marginal same-direction call with `|prob_up - 0.50| < 0.04` to `0.50`.
11. Apply the Geography and Broad-Regime hard gates BEFORE finalizing any down call.

## Confidence Calibration
1. Confidence measures signal quality, not conviction.
2. Confidence above `0.55` requires a fresh hard catalyst plus confirming price action plus confirming volume.
3. Confidence above `0.60` is prohibited unless catalyst is exceptional (takeover, fraud, guidance withdrawal, major dilution, regulatory ban, severe earnings shock).
4. If rationale includes stale, thematic, duplicated, indirect, low-volume, or mixed evidence, cap confidence at `0.50`.
5. **Confidence does not predict accuracy.** Do not raise confidence for familiar tickers, retrieved similar cases, or a strong narrative; the highest-confidence call of the period (down 0.45 / conf 0.53) was wrong by +4.7%.
6. If confidence is `0.50` or lower and there is no fresh hard catalyst, compress final `prob_up` to `0.49-0.51`.

## Required Checklist
Before issuing a prediction, classify:
1. Fresh hard catalyst: `positive`, `negative`, `mixed`, or `none`.
2. Catalyst state: `untraded`, `confirmed`, `faded`, `stale`, or `duplicated`.
3. News novelty: `fresh`, `repeated`, `stale`, or `thematic`.
4. Price action: `confirming up/down`, `reversal up/down`, `failed gap-up`, or `mixed`.
5. Extension: `extended up`, `extended down`, or `not extended`.
6. Sector breadth: `supportive`, `hostile`, `mixed`, or `unknown`.
7. Volume: `confirming`, `neutral`, or `low/uncertain`.
8. **Geography:** `US`, `China/HK`, or `other-Asia` (semi/AI/optical?).
9. **Prior-session regime:** `normal`, `broad-up`, or `broad-washout`.
10. Final override: `hard catalyst`, `post-catalyst fade`, `geography gate`, `washout-bounce gate`, `broad-up gate`, `strong-breadth suppression`, `extension fade`, `oversold rebound`, or `none`.

A down call requires ALL of: not blocked by any Geography/Broad-Regime gate; at least two independent checklist items pointing down; and (for `0.44-0.47`) a fresh negative hard catalyst or confirmed failed gap-up with downside volume.

## Recurring Failure Modes To Avoid
1. Do NOT short Chinese/HK/Asian semis off a hostile US overnight tape — the single largest loss source this period.
2. Do NOT issue continuation down-calls the session after a broad washout — the bounce wins.
3. Do NOT turn a same-day strong-breadth short-suppression into an up tilt on an extended US name; set `0.50`.
4. Do NOT chase stale AI/optics/nuclear/rare-earth/space/data-center narratives after a large top-quartile close.
5. Do NOT chase day-2/day-3 post-earnings winners after a large first reaction without a new measurable update.
6. Do NOT short dilution names on Day 2-3 once extended down (squeeze risk).
7. Do NOT raise confidence when probability is near neutral or because a name/case feels familiar.
8. Do NOT treat product launches or analyst notes as hard catalysts without financial scale.
9. DO take the demonstrated edge: fresh-negative-catalyst US single-name shorts with confirming downside volume on a non-washout tape.
