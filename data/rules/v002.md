# Prediction Rules v002

## Objective And Horizon
- Predict the next trading session's close-to-close direction only. Treat catalysts as valid only if they plausibly affect the next 1 trading day, not the next quarter or year.
- Use `prob_up` as the probability that the stock closes higher next session. A down prediction is any `prob_up < 0.50`; an up prediction is any `prob_up > 0.50`.

## Catalyst Ranking
1. Rank catalysts by this order for 1-day predictions:
   - Fresh earnings results, guidance, or confirmed revenue/margin impact.
   - Fresh company-specific operational news with measurable financial implications.
   - Fresh sector read-throughs from a direct peer reporting earnings or guidance.
   - Broad macro, rates, futures, geopolitical, or sector risk-on/risk-off pressure.
   - Analyst target changes, opinion pieces, product mentions, institutional-positioning articles, and repeated thematic AI headlines.
2. Do not treat product launches, partnerships, MOUs, index inclusion, analyst target hikes, or “AI beneficiary” articles as strong 1-day catalysts unless the news includes explicit near-term revenue, order, margin, or guidance impact.
3. Repeated headlines about the same underlying event count as one catalyst. Multiple duplicate articles must not increase probability or confidence.
4. Bullish AI, data-center, semiconductor, nuclear-power, or cloud narratives are weak 1-day signals when they are already consensus themes. They require price confirmation before supporting an up call.

## Freshness
1. Give maximum weight to catalysts from the last 24 hours.
2. Earnings and guidance catalysts remain active for up to 3 trading sessions, but decay each day:
   - Day 0-1: strong signal if price confirms.
   - Day 2-3: moderate signal only.
   - After day 3: background context, not a primary reason.
3. If a bullish earnings catalyst has already been followed by a large rally and then a weak close, classify the setup as “post-catalyst fade risk,” not bullish continuation.
4. If a bearish catalyst is older than 3 trading sessions and price has stabilized or reversed, do not keep predicting downside solely from that catalyst.

## Price Action Filters
1. When news is bullish but the stock closes down or near the session low, lean down unless there is a fresh hard catalyst after the close.
2. When news is bearish but the stock closes up or near the session high, lean up unless there is a fresh hard negative catalyst after the close.
3. A failed gap-up, intraday reversal, or close in the bottom 25% of the daily range is a bearish 1-day signal.
4. A reversal from a multi-day selloff followed by a close in the top 25% of the daily range is a bullish 1-day signal.
5. Do not predict continuation after a 2-4 session sharp selloff with only stale bearish news; explicitly account for rebound risk.
6. Do not predict continuation after a 2-4 session sharp rally with only stale bullish news; explicitly account for profit-taking risk.

## Stale Bullish Narrative Trap
1. If the rationale is mostly “AI demand,” “data-center demand,” “analyst support,” “institutional interest,” or “long-term market opportunity,” cap `prob_up` at `0.54` unless there is fresh earnings/guidance or strong price confirmation.
2. If stale bullish narratives are paired with weak price action, prefer a down call in the `0.44-0.48` range.
3. This rule is grounded in repeated misses on bullish narrative calls such as COHR, LITE, LRCX, ORCL, GOOGL, AVGO, and TSM when stale positive headlines failed to overcome weak tape.

## Oversold Rebound Trap
1. After three or more consecutive down sessions or a decline greater than 6% over 3 sessions, do not assign `prob_up < 0.45` unless there is a fresh company-specific negative catalyst.
2. If the stock is extended downward but bearish news is stale or low-novelty, keep the call near neutral: `0.46-0.52`.
3. This rule addresses misses where bearish momentum calls failed on rebounds, including SMCI, SMTC, VST, VRT, META, and MTSI on May 21.

## Direct Peer Read-Throughs
1. A fresh earnings or guidance read-through from a direct peer may support a prediction for one session.
2. Apply peer read-throughs only when the business linkage is direct:
   - Nvidia earnings can affect AI semiconductors, AI servers, optics, and infrastructure names.
   - Semiconductor equipment outlook can affect AMAT, ASML, KLAC, LRCX.
   - AI server demand can affect DELL, SMCI, AVGO, MRVL, CRDO, COHR.
3. Cap peer-read-through-only predictions at `0.57` unless the target stock also has confirming price action.
4. Do not let a broad peer read-through override a target stock’s strong opposite price action.

## Macro And Sector Regime
1. When multiple same-day macro/sector signals align against a high-multiple growth or semiconductor stock, they can outweigh stale company-specific bullish news.
2. Macro/sector pressure is strongest when at least two of the following are present:
   - Higher Treasury yields.
   - Hot inflation data.
   - Weak futures or broad tech selloff.
   - Geopolitical risk affecting oil, Taiwan, China, or exports.
   - Sector leader weakness.
3. Macro alone should rarely justify `prob_up < 0.44` or `prob_up > 0.56`.
4. If macro pressure and price action point the same way, allow a modest directional call in the `0.44-0.47` or `0.53-0.56` range.

## Analyst And Institutional Signals
1. Analyst price target changes, rating reiterations, “top pick” calls, and valuation articles are lagging indicators.
2. Use analyst signals only as supporting evidence, never as the primary catalyst, unless the action is a major downgrade/upgrade from a highly influential firm and arrives within 24 hours.
3. Institutional stake headlines support sentiment but are not hard catalysts. Cap probability impact at 2 percentage points.
4. Do not count multiple analyst articles saying the same thing as independent confirmation.

## Probability Calibration
1. Default to `0.50` when evidence is mixed, stale, duplicated, or mostly thematic.
2. Use `0.53-0.57` for modest bullish edges with at least one fresh catalyst or clear positive price confirmation.
3. Use `0.43-0.47` for modest bearish edges with weak price action, stale bullish narratives, or macro pressure.
4. Use `0.58-0.62` only when a fresh hard catalyst and price action agree.
5. Do not use probabilities above `0.62` or below `0.38` for 1-day predictions unless there is an exceptional event such as earnings shock, guidance withdrawal, fraud/accounting issue, takeover, regulatory ban, or major confirmed contract.
6. If the predicted edge depends mainly on mean reversion, keep probability between `0.47` and `0.53`.

## Confidence Calibration
1. Confidence must measure signal quality, not strength of opinion.
2. Assign confidence above `0.58` only when at least three independent signals agree:
   - Fresh company-specific hard catalyst.
   - Confirming price action.
   - Sector or macro alignment.
   - Volume confirmation.
3. If volume is low, partial, or explicitly uncertain, cap confidence at `0.48`.
4. If the rationale includes “low novelty,” “stale,” “duplicated,” “indirect,” or “mixed,” cap confidence at `0.50`.
5. Do not assign confidence above `0.55` to a prediction that is primarily based on fading stale news or extrapolating recent price momentum.
6. Review-period evidence showed confidence was not reliably correlated with accuracy: several high-confidence bearish calls on May 21 missed due to rebounds. Therefore, high confidence requires stricter confirmation than before.

## Volume Rules
1. Elevated volume confirms the direction of a breakout, breakdown, reversal, or post-earnings move.
2. Low volume reduces both confidence and probability magnitude.
3. A low-volume rally after a selloff is not enough for an aggressive up call, but it is enough to avoid an aggressive down call.
4. A low-volume decline after a rally is not enough for an aggressive down call, but it is enough to avoid an aggressive up call.

## Handling Strong Prior Moves
1. If a stock has moved more than 8% over the prior 3 trading sessions, treat it as extended.
2. For extended stocks, require a fresh hard catalyst to predict continuation.
3. Without a fresh hard catalyst:
   - Extended up move: lean neutral-to-down.
   - Extended down move: lean neutral-to-up.
4. Do not combine “extended” and “same-direction momentum” to create a high-confidence call; extension increases reversal risk.

## Sector-Specific Heuristics
1. Semiconductors and AI infrastructure names frequently reverse after crowded bullish headline clusters. Require price confirmation before bullish calls.
2. Semiconductor equipment names respond better to direct WFE outlook, earnings, or export-rule changes than to generic AI demand articles.
3. Optical networking and AI connectivity names are highly momentum-sensitive; after large rallies, product/news headlines alone should be faded or capped near neutral.
4. Power and nuclear/data-center electricity names can continue selling despite strong earnings if valuation pressure dominates price action. However, after multi-day selloffs, downgrade confidence in further downside unless fresh negative news appears.
5. Mega-cap tech names require company-specific catalysts for directional conviction; broad AI optimism or institutional ownership headlines alone should stay close to neutral.

## Similar Case Retrieval
1. Use retrieved similar cases only if they match all three conditions:
   - Same ticker or highly similar business model.
   - Same catalyst class.
   - Same price-action state: rally, selloff, reversal, or consolidation.
2. Do not use retrieved cases as a primary reason when current fresh news or price action clearly differs.
3. If similar cases repeatedly show bullish narrative setups failing after weak price action, apply the stale bullish narrative rule.
4. If similar cases repeatedly show bearish momentum setups failing after extended selloffs, apply the oversold rebound rule.

## Required Prediction Checklist
Before issuing any prediction, classify each item explicitly:

1. Fresh hard catalyst:
   - `positive`, `negative`, or `none`.
2. News novelty:
   - `fresh`, `repeated`, `stale`, or `thematic`.
3. Price action:
   - `confirming up`, `confirming down`, `reversal up`, `reversal down`, or `mixed`.
4. Extension:
   - `extended up`, `extended down`, or `not extended`.
5. Macro/sector backdrop:
   - `supportive`, `hostile`, or `mixed`.
6. Volume:
   - `confirming`, `low/uncertain`, or `neutral`.

Only increase probability away from `0.50` when at least two checklist items point in the same direction. Only increase confidence when those items are independent.

## Common Failure Modes To Avoid
1. Do not chase bullish AI headlines after a large rally and weak close.
2. Do not keep pressing bearish calls after a large multi-day selloff unless new negative information appears.
3. Do not treat analyst target hikes as fresh fundamental evidence.
4. Do not treat duplicate headlines as multiple signals.
5. Do not overrule price action with medium-term strategic narratives.
6. Do not assign high confidence when the rationale itself says the evidence is stale, indirect, low-volume, or mixed.
7. Do not confuse correct direction with good calibration: a `0.57` probability on weak evidence is too aggressive even if it happens to hit.

## Calibration Review Targets
- Maintain most routine predictions between `0.44` and `0.57`.
- Average confidence should fall when signals are low-volume, stale, or indirect.
- Track misses by category after each review:
  - Stale bullish narrative miss.
  - Oversold rebound miss.
  - Macro override miss.
  - Analyst-signal overweight miss.
  - Price-action overfit miss.
- Revise rules only when the same miss category appears at least three times in the review window.
